Over 50 dead in Himachal rain fury; landslides in Uttarakhand, buildings damaged

As on 14 August 2023 as many as 50 people were killed in rain-related incidents in Himachal Pradesh, while heavy downpours in Uttarakhand triggered landslides and caused several rivers to swell. Schools and colleges were closed in these states.Incessant rainfall battered the hill states of Uttarakhand and Himachal Pradesh, throwing normal life out of gear. At least 50 people died in rain-related incidents in Himachal Pradesh, Chief Minister Sukhvinder Singh Sukhu said, as heavy rains continued to pound the state. In neighbouring Uttarakhand, torrential downpours triggered landslides, washing away bridges, forcing the closure of educational institutions and causing water levels of rivers to swell.

As the northern state of Uttarakhand continues to witness monsoon fury, India Meteorological Department on August 09 issued a red alert for various parts of the hilly state starting from today to August 12. The ‘red alert’ areas include Nainital, Champawat, Udham Singh Nagar, and Pauri District. Apart from that, an orange alert with heavy to very heavy rainfall has been alerted in areas of Dehradun, Tehri, and Almora.
